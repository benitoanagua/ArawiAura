Lines of code flow  
like digital rivers—  
each function a verse,  
each bug a mystery  
waiting to be solved.

The screen glows blue,  
illuminating faces  
hunched over keyboards,  
fingers dancing on keys  
creating worlds from nothing.

I write poetry in Python,  
paint with pixels,  
sculpt with algorithms—  
the computer my chisel,  
the internet my gallery.

Debugging is editing,  
refactoring is revision,  
deployment is publication—  
the creative process unchanged  
since cavemen first drew  
on stone walls.

In this binary garden,  
we plant seeds of logic,  
watch them grow into forests  
of possibility,  
where art and science  
finally understand  
they speak the same language.
